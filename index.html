About the Master

The Renaissance Genius

Michelangelo Buonarroti (1475-1564)

Born in Caprese, Italy, Michelangelo di Lodovico Buonarroti Simoni was a sculptor, painter, architect, and poet of the High Renaissance. His mastery of human anatomy and his ability to express emotion through marble revolutionized Western art.

Early Training

Apprenticed to Domenico Ghirlandaio at age 13, then studied sculpture in the Medici gardens under Bertoldo di Giovanni, where he was exposed to classical antiquities.

Artistic Philosophy

Believed that every block of marble contained a sculpture waiting to be revealed. His approach was to "free the figure imprisoned in the marble."

Legacy

His works, including David, Pietà, and the Sistine Chapel ceiling, are considered among the greatest achievements in human artistic history and continue to inspire artists worldwide.
